History


Forming in 1965, The Grateful Dead performed over 2,300 live performances in their career.

The band originally formed in Palo Alto, California in 1965. When they first started playing they went under the name The Warlocks.

They played their first show as The Grateful Dead in San Jose, California in December 1965.

The band had a loyal group of people that would follow them from show to show trying to gain access

any way that they could. Since Grateful Dead fans would follow the band for many months at a time

a lot of people would sell things in the parking lot before the show would start.

Some of the things that would be for sale are things like food, shirts, art, and other more hidden dealings.

The band was known for their long improvisation pieces that would take place at every show

Even though many of the band members wrote numerous top hits the band had, one of their top lyricist was a man that wasn't even a formal member.

Robert Hunter did perform with the band in September 1967, even though it was only for four gigs his lyrics are what made him a special

part of the band
